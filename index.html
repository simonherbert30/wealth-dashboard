Why VWCE is the engine

One ticker, ~3.760 companies, ~90% of the world's market cap
  • The whole market in one line. ~3.760 large & mid‑cap companies across ~49 countries, developed and emerging — roughly 90% of the world's investable value. You're not betting on a company, sector, or country; you own all of them.
  • Self‑cleansing, market‑cap weighted. Winners grow their weight automatically, losers shrink and drop out. No manager to pick wrong, no rebalancing to do — the index maintains itself.
  • Very low cost. 0,19% TER = about €19/year per €10.000. Costs are the one thing you control, and this is near the floor for global equity.
  • Accumulating = tax‑efficient in Spain. Dividends are reinvested inside the fund, so there's no yearly dividend to declare — the taxable event is deferred until you sell, and compounding runs untouched.
  • Irish domicile. 15% US dividend withholding via treaty (vs 30% otherwise) and UCITS investor protections.
  • The catch is behavioural. It only delivers if you sit through a 30–50% drawdown without selling — which is exactly what the discipline rules below are for.

The discipline

The rule that makes everything above work
⊘

No selling

Not on a −30% drop, not on a scary headline, not to "lock in gains." The only exit is a real life need years out.

01

A drawdown isn't a loss.VWCE at −30% is a paper number. It only becomes a loss the moment you sell — so don't.

02

Rebalance with new money only.Steer future contributions. Never sell a winner to adjust.

03

Mute the noise.Kill price alerts. Review quarterly, decide annually. Daily checking is the enemy.

04

Keep a buffer.A liquid emergency fund means a shock never forces a sale at the bottom.

05

The market moving isn't a reason to act.Only your life changing changes the plan.

06

Write down why you'd ever sell.If it's not on that short list, the answer is no.
